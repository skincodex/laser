Comprehensive Medical Contraindications Assessment

DERMATOLOGICAL CONDITIONS - ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS
Active Skin Malignancies:
  • Basal cell carcinoma (in/around treatment area)
  • Squamous cell carcinoma
  • Malignant melanoma
  • Merkel cell carcinoma
  • Cutaneous lymphoma
  • Kaposi's sarcoma
Active Infections:
  • Bacterial cellulitis
  • Impetigo
  • Active herpes simplex (HSV-1/HSV-2)
  • Herpes zoster (shingles)
  • Fungal infections (candida, tinea)
  • Molluscum contagiosum
  • Warts (HPV)
Scarring & Healing Disorders:
  • Keloid scarring history
  • Hypertrophic scarring tendency
  • Ehlers-Danlos syndrome
  • Marfan syndrome
  • Osteogenesis imperfecta
  • Previous poor wound healing
HEMATOLOGICAL & COAGULATION DISORDERS
Bleeding Disorders:
  • Hemophilia A (Factor VIII deficiency)
  • Hemophilia B (Factor IX deficiency)
  • von Willebrand disease (severe)
  • Severe thrombocytopenia (<50,000)
  • Platelet function disorders
  • Moderate thrombocytopenia (50,000-100,000)
Blood Cancers:
  • Active leukemia
  • Lymphoma (Hodgkin's/Non-Hodgkin's)
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Myelodysplastic syndrome
  • Aplastic anemia
  • Polycythemia vera
Anticoagulation Status:
  • Therapeutic anticoagulation (INR >3.0)
  • Dual antiplatelet therapy
  • Recent anticoagulant initiation (<1 month)
  • Low-dose aspirin monotherapy
IMMUNOLOGICAL & AUTOIMMUNE CONDITIONS
Primary Immunodeficiencies:
  • Severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID)
  • Common variable immunodeficiency
  • DiGeorge syndrome
  • Chronic granulomatous disease
  • Selective IgA deficiency
Secondary Immunosuppression:
  • HIV/AIDS (CD4 <200)
  • Active chemotherapy treatment
  • Organ transplant recipients
  • Bone marrow transplant recipients
  • High-dose steroid therapy (>20mg prednisolone)
  • Biologic therapy (anti-TNF agents)
Autoimmune Connective Tissue Diseases:
  • Systemic lupus erythematosus
  • Systemic sclerosis (scleroderma)
  • Dermatomyositis
  • Mixed connective tissue disease
  • Sjögren's syndrome
  • Rheumatoid arthritis (well-controlled)
ENDOCRINE & METABOLIC CONDITIONS
Diabetes Mellitus:
  • Uncontrolled diabetes (HbA1c >9%)
  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Diabetic nephropathy
  • History of diabetic foot ulcers
  • Well-controlled diabetes (HbA1c <7%)
  • Recent diabetic ketoacidosis
Thyroid Disorders:
  • Hyperthyroidism (untreated)
  • Graves' disease
  • Hypothyroidism (untreated)
  • Hashimoto's thyroiditis
  • Recent thyroid surgery
  • Thyroid cancer (active treatment)
Other Endocrine Conditions:
  • Addison's disease
  • Cushing's syndrome
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome
  • Growth hormone disorders
CARDIOVASCULAR CONDITIONS
Cardiac Device Patients:
  • Pacemaker (within 15cm of device)
  • Implantable cardioverter defibrillator (ICD)
  • Cardiac resynchronization therapy device
  • Heart valve replacements (mechanical/tissue)
Vascular Conditions:
  • Severe peripheral arterial disease
  • Chronic venous insufficiency
  • Active deep vein thrombosis
  • Pulmonary embolism (recent)
  • Varicose veins
  • Lymphedema
Cardiac Conditions:
  • Unstable angina
  • Recent myocardial infarction (<3 months)
  • Congestive heart failure (stable)
  • Hypertension (well-controlled)
NEUROLOGICAL & PSYCHIATRIC CONDITIONS
Seizure Disorders:
  • Photosensitive epilepsy
  • Controlled epilepsy
  • Recent seizure activity (<6 months)
  • Medication non-compliance
Neurodegenerative Diseases:
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Alzheimer's disease
  • Myasthenia gravis
Psychiatric Conditions:
  • Active psychosis
  • Severe anxiety disorders
  • Body dysmorphic disorder
  • Trichotillomania/dermatillomania
RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS
Chronic Respiratory Diseases:
  • Severe COPD
  • Severe asthma (uncontrolled)
  • Pulmonary fibrosis
  • Well-controlled asthma
Active Respiratory Conditions:
  • Active respiratory infection
  • Recent pneumonia (<1 month)
  • Chronic cough
RENAL & HEPATIC CONDITIONS
Kidney Disease:
  • Chronic kidney disease (Stage 4-5)
  • Dialysis patients
  • Kidney transplant recipients
  • Mild-moderate CKD (Stage 2-3)
Liver Disease:
  • Severe liver cirrhosis
  • Active hepatitis (A, B, C)
  • Liver transplant recipients
  • Moderate hepatic impairment
  • Gilbert's syndrome
PIGMENTARY & GENETIC DISORDERS
Pigmentation Disorders:
  • Vitiligo
  • Melasma/chloasma
  • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
  • Café-au-lait spots
  • Albinism
Genetic Photosensitivity Syndromes:
  • Xeroderma pigmentosum
  • Porphyria cutanea tarda
  • Polymorphic light eruption
  • Solar urticaria
  • Hydroa vacciniforme
SPECIAL POPULATIONS & SITUATIONS
Age-Related Considerations:
  • Age <16 years
  • Age >75 years
  • Frailty syndrome
  • Multiple comorbidities
Recent Procedures:
  • Recent surgery (<6 weeks) in treatment area
  • Recent cosmetic procedures
  • Recent radiation therapy
  • Recent vaccination (local reaction)
Lifestyle Factors:
  • Heavy smoking (>20/day)
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Poor nutritional status
  • High stress levels
Medical Assessment Protocol
  1. Comprehensive Medical History: Review past and current medical conditions, surgeries, hospitalizations
  2. Systems Review: Cardiovascular, respiratory, neurological, endocrine, hematological assessment
  3. Family History: Genetic conditions, keloid formation, autoimmune diseases
  4. Specialist Consultations: Obtain clearance for complex medical conditions
  5. Laboratory Results: Review recent blood work, imaging studies if relevant
  6. Risk Stratification: Categorize overall medical risk profile
  7. Documentation: Detailed record of all conditions assessed
  8. Monitoring Plan: Establish appropriate follow-up protocols
Clinical Assessment Guidelines:
Perform systematic review of all body systems. When multiple conditions are present, consider cumulative risk. Always obtain specialist clearance for complex medical conditions before proceeding with treatment. Document decision-making rationale for all high-risk cases.

Skin Assessment Factors
  • Recent sun exposure or tanning (within 4-6 weeks)
  • Self-tanning products used recently
  • Active sunburn or irritated skin
  • Skin types V-VI require modified parameters
  • Freckles or age spots in treatment area
  • Previous adverse reactions to laser treatment
Assessment Protocol:
Document skin type, recent sun exposure, and any pigmentary changes. Test patch recommended for skin types IV-VI and uncertain cases.

Tattoo Characteristics Assessment

Laser-Responsive vs Difficult Colors
Easier to Remove:
  • Black ink (all laser types)
  • Dark blue
  • Red (532nm laser)
  • Brown
Difficult to Remove:
  • Yellow and fluorescent colors
  • White and light colors
  • Purple
  • Green (may require specific wavelengths)
Tattoo-Related Considerations
  • Professional vs amateur tattoo (depth varies)
  • Age of tattoo (older may be easier)
  • Location on body (circulation affects healing)
  • Previous unsuccessful laser treatments
  • Cover-up tattoos (multiple ink layers)
  • Size and density of tattoo
  • Metallic inks (may cause darkening)
Treatment Planning:
Document ink colors, tattoo age, previous treatments, and realistic expectations. Some colors may require specialized wavelengths or may not respond to treatment.

Medication Review Protocol
  1. Comprehensive History: Review all prescription medications, OTC drugs, supplements, and herbal remedies
  2. Timing Assessment: Determine when medications were last taken and duration of use
  3. Risk Stratification: Categorize each medication by photosensitivity and bleeding risk
  4. Prescriber Consultation: Contact prescribing physician for high-risk medications
  5. Discontinuation Planning: Develop safe withdrawal timelines where appropriate
  6. Alternative Assessment: Consider safer alternatives during treatment period
  7. Documentation: Record all medications, risks assessed, and actions taken
  8. Follow-up: Schedule medication review before each subsequent session
Critical Medication Protocol:
Never advise patients to discontinue prescribed medications without prescriber consultation. For life-saving medications (cardiac, seizure, organ transplant), treatment postponement may be necessary. Always document medication review discussions and obtain written clearance for high-risk cases.

Lifestyle & Environmental Factors

Lifestyle Assessment
  • Recent sun exposure or planned sun exposure
  • Smoking (impairs wound healing)
  • Alcohol consumption (affects healing)
  • Occupational sun exposure
  • Exercise routine (sweating may affect healing)
  • Swimming/water activities post-treatment
  • Stress levels (may affect healing)
Pre-Treatment Lifestyle Requirements:
  • No sun exposure 4-6 weeks prior to treatment
  • Discontinue self-tanning products 2 weeks before
  • Stay hydrated and maintain good nutrition
  • Avoid alcohol 24-48 hours before treatment
  • Ensure adequate rest before appointment

Pregnancy & Breastfeeding

Pregnancy Status - ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION
  • Confirmed pregnancy (any trimester)
  • Suspected pregnancy
  • Trying to conceive
  • Breastfeeding (relative contraindication)
Pregnancy Protocol:
Always ask about pregnancy status and last menstrual period. Consider pregnancy test if uncertain. Treatment should be postponed until after pregnancy and breastfeeding completion.

Post-Treatment Care Requirements

Essential Aftercare Instructions
  • Apply cold compress immediately post-treatment
  • Keep treatment area clean and dry
  • Apply prescribed topical antibiotic if indicated
  • Avoid sun exposure for 4-6 weeks
  • Use SPF 30+ sunscreen when outdoors
  • No picking or scratching treated area
  • Avoid swimming/hot tubs for 48-72 hours
  • No vigorous exercise for 24-48 hours
Warning Signs - Seek Immediate Medical Attention
  • Signs of infection (pus, red streaking, fever)
  • Severe or worsening pain after 48 hours
  • Unexpected blistering or ulceration
  • Allergic reaction symptoms
  • Pigmentary changes beyond expected

Informed Consent Checklist

Required Consent Elements:
  • Nature of laser tattoo removal procedure explained
  • Expected number of sessions discussed
  • Potential risks and complications outlined
  • Alternative treatment options considered
  • Cost and payment terms agreed
  • Aftercare instructions provided in writing
  • Before/after photographs consent obtained
  • Right to withdraw consent acknowledged
  • Emergency contact information provided
  • Follow-up appointment scheduled
Documentation Requirements:
Maintain detailed records including medical history, assessment findings, treatment parameters, client consent, and any adverse reactions. Ensure all documentation meets regulatory standards.
