Laser Tattoo Removal - Post-Treatment Care Instructions
🚨 CALL YOUR PROVIDER IMMEDIATELY IF:
Infection signs: increasing redness, warmth, pus, red streaking, fever • Severe allergic reaction: widespread rash, difficulty breathing • Excessive blistering or severe pain not controlled by medication
FIRST 24 HOURS
✓ DO
  • Apply ice packs 10-15 min/hour (wrapped in cloth)
  • Keep clean and dry
  • Take pain medication as directed
  • Rest and elevate treated area if possible
✗ DON'T
  • Pick, scratch, or rub treated area
  • Apply makeup or lotions to area
  • Direct sun exposure
  • Hot showers, exercise, or alcohol
DAILY WOUND CARE
• Wash hands before touching treated area
• Gently clean with mild soap and water
• Pat dry (don't rub) with clean towel
• Apply thin layer of prescribed ointment
• Cover if directed - repeat 2-3 times daily
Pain Management: Ibuprofen 400-600mg every 6-8hrs OR Acetaminophen 500-1000mg every 6hrs. AVOID aspirin.
HEALING TIMELINE & ACTIVITIES
Days 1-3
  • Redness & swelling normal
  • Possible blistering
  • NO exercise or swimming
Days 4-14
  • Scabbing begins
  • Itching may start
  • Light exercise OK
Weeks 2-8
  • Scabs fall off naturally
  • Gradual fading continues
  • Resume normal activities
SUN PROTECTION - MANDATORY
Avoid sun exposure 4+ weeks. Use SPF 30+ after complete healing.
  • Cover with clothing when outdoors
  • Stay in shade between 10am-4pm
  • NO tanning beds or sunbathing
OPTIMIZE HEALING
DO:
  • Stay hydrated (8+ glasses water)
  • Eat protein-rich foods
  • Get adequate sleep (7-9hrs)
AVOID:
  • Smoking and alcohol
  • Picking at scabs
  • Harsh soaps/products
⚠️ WHAT TO EXPECT
Normal: Mild redness, swelling, blistering, scabbing
CONCERNING: Increasing pain after day 3, pus, foul odor
EMERGENCY: Red streaking, fever, severe allergic reaction
NEXT TREATMENT PREPARATION
Wait 6-8 weeks minimum between sessions. Complete healing required.
  • Ensure no scabs or irritation before next session
  • Avoid sun exposure 2 weeks before treatment
  • Report any complications to your provider
